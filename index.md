Hello! My name is Tyler. I am a software developer. Online, I usually go by _DataKinds_, a name I ripped from [the GHC extension of the same name](https://ghc.gitlab.haskell.org/ghc/doc/users_guide/exts/data_kinds.html){:target="_blank"}. Hence, you've descended into <strong class="funny">DK's ABODE</strong>.

Computers have been part of my life for as long as I can remember. I was in diapers helping my grandmother navigate her Windows 2000 PC to boot her casino games. Shortly after that, I was writing [Roblox Lua](https://create.roblox.com/docs/luau){:target="_blank"} and raking in enough tickets to buy some of the rarest gear on the platform. Don't talk to me about how my account got hacked in 2018. I lost $25k!

Through school, when I wasn't doing theatre and keeping up with coursework, [I studied the blade](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/i-studied-the-blade){:target="_blank"}. I [became an enthusiast code-golfer, wielding Ruby as my tool of choice](https://codegolf.stackexchange.com/users/19339/aearnus){:target="_blank"}. I learned a whole slew of programming languages: not just Ruby, but Python, Haskell, C, and a handful of APLs --- J first, then BQN later. 

At the University of Arizona I bounced between a few things but decided to major in Mathematics and minor in Theatre. I graduated with this degree cum laude in 2021. During my degree I got the unique opportunity to work with Hydrant, a Ruby on Rails software house serving the local community. After that it was [Firefly Drone Shows](https://www.fireflydroneshows.com/){:target="_blank"}, a startup out of Michigan that put on extensive light shows across the country. I stuck with Firefly for almost 6 years and got the unique opportunity to really own a software stack that was vital for a company. To put it lightly, I caught the bug for management and haven't shaken it since.

Since then I've worked with various companies on contract in the financial sector. I function best drinking from a firehose as long as I have the proper structural support, and both American Express and Coinbase provided that for me. With the former I learned risk management and event-driven development, with the latter I learned AI-first workflows and service-level optimization. [Somewhere along the line I took a liking to Golang --- what a crazy little gopher!](/assets/golang-my-beloved.gif)

In my free time I study programming language theory. I have [multiple](https://github.com/DataKinds/charm){:target="_blank"} different [languages](https://github.com/DataKinds/tree-rewriter){:target="_blank"} that I've [experimented](https://github.com/DataKinds/sixty-five-oh-two){:target="_blank"}  with over the years. I'm [always looking](https://github.com/DataKinds/apl?tab=readme-ov-file#goals-and-non-goals){:target="_blank"} to [push the boundaries](https://datakinds.github.io/2019/06/30/jamq-thinkbox){:target="_blank"} of what's possible. When I'm not studying the above, I like to ride my skateboard, watch motorsports, do some woodworking, and play video games with my fiancée.
